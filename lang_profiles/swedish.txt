# Language Background: Swedish

## Similarities to Danish
- Danish and Swedish are mutually intelligible in written form -- a huge advantage.
- Shared vocabulary is very high: "hus/hus", "vatten/vand", "vinter/vinter", "broed/broed".
- Both have two grammatical genders (common/neuter): Swedish "en/ett" mirrors Danish "en/et".
- Both attach the definite article as a suffix: Swedish "huset" = Danish "huset".
- Verb conjugation is similarly simple -- no person agreement.

## Key Differences to Highlight
- Danish pronunciation is the main challenge: softer, more reduced, and less melodic than Swedish.
- Danish has the stoed (glottal stop) -- Swedish does not; Swedish has tonal word accent instead.
- The soft d in Danish has no direct Swedish equivalent.
- Some false friends exist: Swedish "rolig" (fun) vs Danish "rolig" (calm/quiet).
- Danish spelling occasionally diverges: Swedish "och" = Danish "og", Swedish "vad" = Danish "hvad".

## Teaching Tips for Swedish Speakers
- Start by reading Danish -- comprehension will come quickly.
- Focus the bulk of effort on spoken Danish pronunciation, especially vowel reduction and the soft d.
- Use Swedish cognates as anchors, but flag the false friends early.
- The tonal instinct from Swedish will not transfer -- replace it with awareness of the stoed.
